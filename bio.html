personal bio

I am a y/o researcher in Computing and Physics. I grew up in India, mostly in the northern plains in Lucknow and later, in the lower Himalayas in Dehradun. After college, I moved to Geneva, Switzerland where I worked in the experimental physics department at CERN. Soon after that, I moved to Delft, Netherlands to continue my Masters. I worked on experimental aspects of enabling a scalable implementation of Quantum Processors. After graduating I continued on a parallel track of exploring theoretical quantum information devising quantum algorithms for High-Energy Physics experiments at LBNL/UC Berkeley and later as Electronics Engineer at CERN. Currently, I work on integrated circuit design for reliable mordern computing and biomedical electronics at École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL).

In my spare time, I work on white hat hacking on networks and systems. I like cycling off-road, bike-packing and hiking. This comes alongside my hobbies as ranging from a nature lover to exploring ancient cities with intriguing historical backgrounds. I am a native Hindi speaker, fluent in English and a moderate-level French speaker.

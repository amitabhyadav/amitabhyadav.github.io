about me

I'm a research fellow at CERN and an affiliate at University of California, Berkeley. My current research includes machine learning from first principles, high-performance hardware/software system co-design, and quantum information theory.

Prior to this, I was a research associate at the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory in the Physics Department, where I worked on hep.qpr project, helping implement combinatorial optimization and pattern recognition algorithms on a quantum computer for the high-energy physics use case. I have obtained my masters in computer engineering from the Delft University of Technology where I specialized in micro-architecture design for the control and read-out of spin-qubit (in quantum-dot) quantum processor at QuTech, the Netherlands.

I am also the cofounder of [ SyncMind | AI ], a full-stack computing and microelectronics design research initiative based in Geneva, Switzerland.
